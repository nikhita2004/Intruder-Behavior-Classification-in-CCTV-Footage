Large amounts of surveillance data captured by CCTV cameras make the task of monitoring them manually difficult, which may lead to delayed response to emergencies and reduction in safety level. The present paper suggests an innovative method for classification and detection of abnormal activity based on a Long-term Recurrent Convolutional Network (LRCN) architecture utilizing MobileNetV2 in combination with TimeDistributed layers and an LSTM unit capable of analyzing videos in sequences of 40 frames and performing temporal sampling at the training phase.
At the stage of designing the model, several CNN architectures have been experimentally evaluated including MobileNetV2, InceptionV3, and EfficientNetB0 in order to find the most appropriate trade-off between accuracy, robustness, and computational speed with respect to real-time use cases; thus, the MobileNetV2 was chosen as the best architecture due to its ability to learn features from surveillance data efficiently. During training, the model was taught with a balanced dataset containing 450 video clips using structured fine-tuning with focal loss and label smoothing, achieving high overall accuracy of 82.2%. Also, the developed algorithm proved to be effective in detecting road accidents and robberies: F1-score for robbery reached 0.86 and 0.87 for road accidents. At the same time, a real-time alerting system was implemented using Telegram Bot API.
